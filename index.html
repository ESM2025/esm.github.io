About

Soft matter materials are present in daily life, especially in foods. As a result, soft matter and foods share many common physical states: gels, emulsions, foams, suspensions and more. We believe that both research fields could combine their forces to better understand and design functional materials, thereby addressing world-wide problems with out-of-the-world solutions.

The purpose of this conference is then to bring together researchers from academia and industry in both soft matter and food science. Scientists in physics, physical-chemistry, and soft matter on one side, and food science on the other side, will have opportunities to exchange ideas and strengthen cooperation.

The congress will be organized around two invited lectures, thematic sessions, a poster session and exhibition of scientific instruments.

Program

More than 90 abstracts have been received. They have been separated in 4 sessions of similar sizes. The selection of orals and posters, in each session, is under progress. A detailed final program will be announced by Mid-April.

Sessions

session 1: Multi-scale structures

session 2: Dispersed systems: emulsions, foams, interfaces

session 3: Phase separation, coacervation, transformation, digestibility, new ingredients

session 4: Processing, characterization, modelling

Monday Short-course program

Young Faculty Networking Event

On Monday morning, the 7th.

Are you an early-career scientist looking to connect with peers at a similar career stage? We invite assistant and associate professors (or equivalent positions) to join us for the young faculty networking event. The event starts in the morning at Rennes university, followed by an afternoon social activity. This is a great opportunity to meet like-minded researchers, pitch your work, and spark potential collaborations in a supportive community. We look forward to welcoming you to the Young Faculty Network in Food Soft Matter!

For more information or to sign up - email jack.yang@wur.nl
